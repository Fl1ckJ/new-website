We're often told by steel companies that they already "digitally sign" their certificates. Curiosity makes us ask: how, exactly?

The usual answer: an authorised person copies and pastes an image of a written signature onto a PDF. In our view that's a digital *image* of a signature — not a digital signature. The receiver can never really verify whether it's genuine.

## What "true digital" means
A true digital signature is cryptographically bound to the data and the signer. Anyone receiving the certificate can verify it hasn't been altered and who approved it — without trusting the sender's word. That's the difference between a document that looks official and a record you can prove.
