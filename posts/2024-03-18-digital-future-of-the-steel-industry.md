In today's fast-paced industry, two developments are quietly revolutionising how quality is proven: digital **Manufacturing Record Books (MRBs)** and the fight against **counterfeit certificates**.

## From paper MRBs to living records
Traditionally, MRBs were paper-based — manual data entry, endless paperwork, and a binder assembled long after production finished. Digital MRBs capture and organise production data in real time, as the work happens, so the record is complete the moment a project closes.

## Closing the door on counterfeit certificates
Counterfeit certificates erode trust across the supply chain and can contribute to project failures. A digital, verifiable record makes a certificate's origin checkable — tracing heat numbers, verifying mills, and binding every approval to a real signature instead of a pasted image.

Some of the benefits teams see almost immediately:

- Higher efficiency and far less manual checking
- Improved, rule-based quality control
- Stronger traceability and data security
- Real cost savings

A trustworthy digital thread also lays the groundwork for accurate carbon accounting and, eventually, Digital Twins.
